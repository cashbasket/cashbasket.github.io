Ryan Machan

Ryan is a full-stack web developer from Cleveland, Ohio. He is passionate about code, creativity, collaboration, and cleverness.

About Ryan

Ryan has worked professionally as a web developer for over 15 years, and has been building websites for longer than that. From marketing-focused websites to social apps to administrative apps, he has worked on a wide variety of things, and has a rather diverse skill set.

Though he has used other technologies over the years, he works primarily with .NET and JavaScript, along with Microsoft SQL Server and MySQL. He truly has a penchant for learning new things, and hopes to continue learning and applying as much as he can.

In his spare time, Ryan is a complete music nerd in every way. He also enjoys creative writing, stand-up/sketch comedy, sci-fi anything, video games of all kinds – except the bad kind – and telling people to watch the first season of Castle Rock on Hulu.

You should watch the first season of Castle Rock on Hulu.

Portfolio

While not all the things Ryan has worked on over the years are currently viewable – and some simply aren't accessible to the public – here's a brief smattering of some public-facing, alive-and-well things Ryan has worked on (or is still working on).

 

Weatherhead School of Management website

This is the official website for the Weatherhead School of Management, Case Western Reserve University. It is aimed primarily at prospective students, but also has numerous resources for current students, faculty, and staff. Ryan has worked with numerous other developers on this site, but has touched pretty much all of it, working on both the front and back end. It is written in ASP.NET (C#).

View the site

 

PROJECT 145

PROJECT 145 is an app that allows people who are really into music – or, perhaps, borderline obsessed with it – to write about music, discuss music with each other, recommend music to each other, and be reasonable while doing so. It was built with JavaScript on the front end (React) and back end (Node, Express), along with a MySQL database. The Spotify API ended up edging out the Discogs API in the "Music API That Should Be Used for PROJECT 145" race, for various reasons.

This is one of Ryan's passion projects, and he has many, many ideas for additions and/or improvements, so expect this app to evolve over time.

View the site | View Ryan's public profile

 

For the Tour

For the Tour is a web app that allows users to find out when and where their favorite bands are touring, as well as RSVP for shows and add shows to their Google calendar. Ryan worked on a small team, and was responsible for the search functionality (using the Songkick API), as well as the RSVP system using Firebase, JavaScript, jQuery, CSS3 and HTML5. He also integrated the Google Calendar, Google Maps, and Google Sign-in APIs.

View the site | View the repo

 

Is This Game Good?

Is This Game Good? was built for two reasons: a) Ryan wanted a way to catalog all the video games he's ever played, and b) he also wanted an excuse to build something with Vue.js. The premise of the site started off as a joke, but ended up as... a joke.

View the site

 

The Simpsons (.gif)

The Simpsons (.gif) started off as an excuse to mess around with the GIPHY API, but eventually turned itself into something Ryan looks at more often than he'd care to admit. It uses a custom-built system for arranging and positioning the images on the screen, because Ryan really wanted to figure out how to do something that looked like this. And yes, he knows about Masonry.js, but wanted a challenge.
