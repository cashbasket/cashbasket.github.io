Ryan is a full-stack web developer from Cleveland, Ohio. He is passionate about innovative code, heavy music, surreal humor, and the pursuit of knowledge.

About Ryan

Ryan Machan has been into coding for a long time. From the day he first learned a few random HTML tags from a high school classmate, Ryan has been consistently working on websites and honing his craft, both for his own enjoyment and [hopefully] the enjoyment of others.

Ryan has worked primarily in the higher education sector, creating sophisticated and user-friendly websites and web applications for use by faculty, staff and students. From public-facing, marketing-focused sites to event registration sites to administrative web apps, he has worked on a wide variety of things over the years. He loves learning new programming languages, frameworks, and concepts in general.

After becoming proficient with frontend design and development, Ryan was introduced to the backend via Coldfusion and Microsoft SQL Server. After that, he learned all about object-oriented programming via C#. These days, he primarily builds sites using ASP.NET (C#) and JavaScript (Node.js) with SQL Server, MySQL, and even Firebase; while backend JavaScript is technically his most recent major discovery, it is probably his most pleasant discovery as well.

In his spare time, Ryan is a complete music nerd in virtually every way; he has probably spent more money on albums than he has on food at this point. He also enjoys horror movies, stand-up comedy, football, sci-fi anything, and video games of all kinds. Oh, and if you need to see an absolutely absurd meme or two (or three), let him know. Seriously.

Portfolio

Websites come and websites go, but here's a brief sample of some public-facing, alive-and-well projects Ryan has worked on recently.

 

Weatherhead School of Management website

This is the official website for the Weatherhead School of Management, Case Western Reserve University. It is aimed primarily at prospective students, but also has numerous resources for current students, faculty, and staff. Ryan has worked on this site for many years with many different people (including external design firms) through many iterations, and has done everything from frontend/backend updates to building out new apps/sections/features to complete site overhauls. It is a beast in terms of size and scope, but it's seen many improvements over the years. It was initially built in raw HTML before Ryan's time - this site goes way back - and then became a Coldfusion website, but is in the process of being ported entirely to ASP.NET.

View the site

 

For the Tour

For the Tour is a web app that allows users to find out when and where their favorite bands are touring, as well as RSVP for shows and add shows to their Google calendar. Ryan worked on a small team, and was responsible for the search functionality (using the Songkick API), as well as the RSVP system using Firebase, JavaScript, jQuery, CSS3 and HTML5. He also integrated the Google Calendar, Google Maps, and Google Sign-in APIs.

View the site | View the GitHub repo

 

The Simpsons (.gif)

The Simpsons (.gif) is a web app that started off as an excuse to mess around with the GIPHY API, but eventually turned itself into something Ryan uses more often than he'd care to admit. It uses a custom-built system for arranging and positioning the images on the screen, because a) Ryan really wanted to figure out how to do something that looked like this, and b) Ryan did not know of the existence of Masonry.js at the time. Even so, it was a lot of fun!

View the site | View the GitHub repo

 

Rock, Paper, Scissors: Multiplayer Edition

Rock, Paper, Scissors: Multiplayer Edition is a web app that allows two users to play "Rock, Paper, Scissors" against each other, and chat (tastefully) while doing so.

View the site | View the GitHub repo

 

Don't Let Chuck Norris Kick You in the Face

Who doesn't love a good, old-fashioned, Norris-themed game? Starting off a perfectly innocent game of "Hangman" meant to test Ryan's frontend Javascript and CSS ability, Don't Let Chuck Norris Kick You in the Face became so much more. If you don't know anything about Chuck, it might be more difficult for you; if you DO know about Chuck... it will probably still be difficult for you.
